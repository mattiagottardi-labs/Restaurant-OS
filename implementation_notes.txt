The current implementation of the different parties work as follows:

CUSTOMER LOOP:
- The customer generates a random order.
- The customer places himself in the customer queue.
- The customer waits until the order is fulfilled, or time runs out.
- If the order is fulfilled, the customer applies bonus and returns.
- If time runs out, the customer joins the dissatified queue.
- The dissatified queue is handled at the end of the simulation, and applies all the malus. 
- Alternatively, if the score is requested at runtime, the malus calculation is done at runtime.


WAITER LOOP:
- The waiter checks the customer queue.
- If there is a customer, the waiter takes the order and places it in the correct order queue. (MLFQ)
note:   order queues are implemented to make the cooks only worry about  
        a reduced pool of orders with the highest priority, and choose the one closest to completion. (SJF)
- The waiter checks the finished orders queue.
- The waiter is responsible for QUEUE SANITIAZATION meaning it must take care of orders whose customers are out of patience for.
- If there is a finished order, the waiter takes it and delivers it to the customer.


COOK LOOP:
- checks the order queue with the highest priority
- chooses the order closest to completion (SJF)
- if no order is available in the queue, checks for the next queue.

The data is organized in the following way:

CUSTOMERS: 
- There is a customer queue where customers wait for their orders to be taken by the waiters.

ORDERS:
- There is an order manager, which contains the order queues (MLFQ) and the finished orders queue.
- The waiters and cooks interact with the order manager to take and place orders.
- The order manager is responsible for managing the order queues and ensuring that orders 
  are processed according to the MLFQ and SJF policies.
- The order manager also handles the synchronization between the different parties, ensuring that
  the waiters and cooks can access the order queues without conflicts.

MENU:
- The menu is a list of dishes, each with a name, preparation time, and price.
- The menu is used by the customers to generate their orders, and by the cooks to know how long it takes to prepare each dish.
- The menu is a global variable that can be accessed by all parties, but it is read-only to prevent conflicts.

Lifetime of the program:
- Start the program, initialize the menu and the order manager.
- Waits for signals that enumarate the number of customer threads, waiter threads, and cook threads.
- The threads will run indefinitely, simulating the restaurant operation.
- The program can be terminated by a signal (e.g., SIGINT) or after a certain amount of time.

SIGNALS:
- The program can handle signals to allow for graceful shutdowns or to trigger certain actions (e.g., print statistics).
- The .sh file is tasked with sending signals to the program to inform it about the number of 
  customers, waiters, and cooks to create, as well as to trigger the termination of the program 
  after a certain time, it can also be used to send signals to print statistics or perform other actions 
  during the program's execution.
